Why is this important?

Quick Question

You are going on a trip to a far away country where you've never been. The flight ticket to get there cost you a lot, leaving you with limited spending money, and with only three days to visit. Which of the following can you do ahead of the trip to ensure you have the best time possible?

  • I'll do my research ahead of time (e.g. talk to people, read books, look online)

    Yes! How else would you know what to do there?

  • I'll prioritize what I want to see/do there ahead of time

    Definitely! This one is about being effective. Since you only have a limited time there, prioritizing will ensure you spend it on the most valuable things first.

  • I'll plan my schedule / itinerary ahead of time

    Right! This one is about being efficient: getting the most out of your limited resources (time, energy, money, etc.)

  • I'll just trust my luck when I get there.

    Probably not. In some cases you may end up having a lot of fun, but you risk missing out on things that are even more fun (or about as fun but less costly), or worse: ending up somewhere you don't want to be.

It's the same with your career

You have limited time, energy, money, and other resources, and you want to make the most of them career-wise.

You need to research your options, decide what's more important and/or more appealing to you, and come up with a plan to make it all happen.

How to know when you've reached it (goals)

A goal is a dream with a deadline.

Goals are how you know you've achieved your vision, and they have to be SMART:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Ambitious
  • Reasonable
  • Time-based

Quick Question

How is the finish line is a SMART goal? (Sort the table cells to match)

SpecificThere's only one finish line for the race, and it's very clear where it is.
MeasurableThe finish line is at exactly a certain distance from the starting line, and winning is determined by who finished the race in the shortest time.
AmbitiousThe finish line with its clock can tell you if you beat the other competitors or broke a record.
ReasonableThe finish line (and indeed the entire race) is designed around the skill level of its participants. For example a marathon course (~26 miles) is not expected to be won in two minutes.
Time-basedYou must reach the finish line within a certain time, at least before they take it down and call it a day.
