Quick Question

Why do new hires spend time onboarding when they first begin a new job? (check all that apply)

  • To acquire the information, resources, and connections they need to be effective in their role, on their team, and at the organization
  • Because if they jump into projects and important work before onboarding, they might make critical mistakes their first days on the job
  • Their expectations may not be aligned to their manager’s expectations for what success looks like
  • To have a slow start and cut you some slack in the first few weeks

    Employers actually appreciate quick learners who can be productive sooner.

  • To prepare for a test that ensures they know everything about the job

    Most workplaces don’t run such tests, so this answer is not universally correct.

  • Because they need to prepare to interview for the job

    Onboarding begins once you’ve already started the job.

Answer

WHAT’S EXPECTED OF YOU

Quick question

When you think of a job/internship you have or a job/internship you’d like to have, what does/will your manager expect of you? (check all that apply)

You will...

  • Come prepared to actively participate in each meeting

  • Manage your projects and meet deadlines

  • Attend work each day and get there on time

  • Seek help from others when you need it

  • Put in your best effort

  • Communicate well ahead of time when you’re running late to a meeting or on a project

  • Work 24/7

    You can't work 24/7 and do a good job! Balance is super important, and good managers understand that.

  • Ace every single project

    You'll fail and fall sometimes, and that's perfectly normal. It's how you learn from your mistakes that show your true worth as an employee.

It just so happens that at Braven, we expect the very same things a manager in a job expects of you.

Quick question

When should you consult a project’s rubric? (check all that apply)

  • Before starting to work on the project, to understand how you’ll be assessed
  • While working on the project, if you have questions
  • Before submitting the project, to ensure you’ve met all the expectations
  • After you receive your graded project, to determine what you did well and what you need to improve

Braven is a career accelerator, so we do focus on professionalism to ensure you feel confident in professional workplaces.

Professionalism at Braven looks like this (the bolded points affect your grade):

  • Attending all Learning Labs and the mandatory Saturday event, Storytelling as Leadership
  • Showing up to all meetings on time
  • Communicating with the Braven team, your Leadership Coach, and the other Fellows as far in advance as possible if you have a conflict and must miss a meeting (no later than 24 hours in advance)

  • Attending office hours when you have questions for the Teaching Assistants/Fellows about projects or your grade

  • Taking full advantage of this experience
